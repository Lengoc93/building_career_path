# Compensation & Negotiation

> The VN band is **not finalized** — this is both risk and leverage. The recruiter's
> anchor is an APAC benchmark, not a committed VN offer. Play it accordingly.

## What we know

- **Total annual comp signal:** ~**VND 3–4B** (≈ **USD 118K–158K** at ~25.4k VND/USD,
  Jul 2026 — verify FX at offer time), **including base + equity**.
- Benchmarked against **similar APAC roles** in **Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong** — i.e.
  a regional-standard number, not a discounted VN-local number. Good sign.
- **VN band not set**; legal entity ~**6 months** out. So there's genuine room to shape.
- Equity is part of the package but **terms unspecified** (%, valuation basis, vesting,
  what "equity" means before the VN entity exists).

## Reading the leverage

**In my favor:**
- They're **urgently** searching leadership for VN, and they **don't understand the VN
  market** — I'm scarce on exactly the axis they're short on (VN enterprise-AI + real
  deployment track record).
- Comp is unfixed → I can influence where the band lands rather than negotiate against a
  rigid grid.
- Company is well-capitalized ($286M raised, $2B valuation) — equity has real potential
  upside; they can afford strong comp.

**Against me / to manage:**
- No committed VN band yet → offer could take time; get specifics before over-investing.
- Pre-entity employment ambiguity → the *structure* (how/where I'm paid, currency, tax,
  benefits) matters as much as the number.
- Equity at a $2B mark = lower % for the same dollar value, and later-stage upside is
  more modest than seed — value it realistically.

## Strategy

1. **Don't anchor first.** When asked expectations early (HR round), reflect their own
   benchmark: *"You mentioned APAC roles benchmark around VND 3–4B total — that's a
   reasonable frame. Before I put a number on it I'd want to understand how base vs.
   equity splits and how equity is structured."* Keeps me in range without capping myself.
2. **Separate the components.** Negotiate **base**, **equity**, and **structure**
   (currency, pre-entity vehicle, benefits, review cadence) as distinct levers. If base
   is constrained pre-entity, push equity and a written comp-review trigger at entity
   formation.
3. **Anchor high within their frame, with justification.** Aim for the **top of the
   VND 3–4B band or above**, justified by: scarcity (VN market intelligence they lack),
   proven 0→1 production deployment, and the founding-market ownership scope.
4. **Get equity specifics in writing:** % or unit count, current valuation basis
   ($2B post-B), vesting schedule + cliff, what happens to equity if I start as
   contractor pre-entity, and refresh policy.
5. **Protect the pre-entity window:** clarify the employment vehicle, payment currency,
   and a **written commitment to re-baseline comp** when the VN entity is established —
   so I'm not locked to an interim number.
6. **Use competing-optionality carefully:** if other pipelines (see `../../../tracker.md`)
   are live, that strengthens my hand — reference optionality, don't bluff.
