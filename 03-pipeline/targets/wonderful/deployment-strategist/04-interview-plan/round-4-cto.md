# Round 4 — Meeting with the CTO

**This is my reporting line.** The CTO is assessing: can I be trusted to own
deployments in-market with minimal hand-holding, do I think in systems, will I
represent engineering reality to customers (and customer reality to engineering), and
am I a builder who fits a hyper-growth startup. This is the most important round for
scope and trust.

## What the CTO most wants to hear

1. **Systems thinking + ownership** — I reduce their load, I don't add to it.
2. **Sound technical judgment** — I won't overpromise to customers or mis-scope integrations.
3. **Bias to build under ambiguity** — greenfield VN, no playbook, and I'm energized by that.
4. **Trustworthy translator** — between customer, Product, and Engineering.

## Talking points tuned to a CTO

- **Deployment as an engineering-leverage function:** "My job is to keep your engineers
  out of low-value customer firefighting. I absorb ambiguity at the customer edge, hand
  Engineering clean requirements and reproducible issues, and protect the roadmap from
  one-off custom asks by turning them into reusable patterns."
- **Guardrails/architecture credibility:** walk the ~9,500-line state machine — 17
  regulatory states embedded in the decision pipeline, dual-LLM reflection,
  reset-and-redetect. This proves I can hold a real architecture conversation and that
  I think about safety structurally.
- **Production-truth orientation:** the 1.7x/5.2x turnaround came from *root-causing a
  live production gap*, not from a deck. CTOs trust people who live in production.
- **Platform vs. bespoke tension:** I understand the startup scaling trap — every market
  wants customization, and the platform must stay one platform. I sequence customer asks
  into reusable capabilities, not snowflakes. (Ties to their "customizable per market"
  design.)

## Questions to ask the CTO (see `06-questions-to-ask.md`)

- "How do you keep localization per market from fragmenting into N bespoke forks?"
- "Where does the Deployment Strategist's authority end and Product's begin on the roadmap?"
- "What does agent quality/eval look like today across languages — and what breaks first
  when you enter a new-language market?"
- "For the VN market pre-GM, how much latitude does this role have to define the local
  playbook?"

## Likely hard probes → my answers

| Probe | Answer spine |
| :---- | :----------- |
| "You've done voice/fintech, not multi-vertical CX." | Deployment-under-complexity transfers; new verticals = new rule sets on a proven platform, not a rebuild. (Same reframe as `02-fit-and-positioning.md`.) |
| "Can you be technical enough for our engineers to respect you?" | I owned a production agent architecture and its eval loop; I know the failure modes. I earn trust by shipping clean scope and honest constraints, not by claiming ML depth I don't have. |
| "This is chaotic and remote — will you thrive without structure?" | I built a 6-market pipeline and a 0→1 production launch under exactly that ambiguity; structure is something I create, not something I need handed to me. |
