# Opportunity Brief — Deployment Strategist @ Wonderful

The single-page truth for this role. Everything below is either from the JD or
directly from the recruiter (see `03-recruiter-intel.md` for verbatim source).

## The role in one line

Lead the end-to-end deployment of Wonderful's AI agents for Tier-1 enterprise
customers in Vietnam — from discovery and pilot scoping through production launch,
adoption, and expansion — bridging customer, Product, and Engineering.

## What the JD says (responsibilities)

- Lead **end-to-end deployment** of AI solutions / enterprise platforms for customers.
- **Scope** requirements, success metrics, and integration plans for pilots and onboardings.
- Coordinate platform implementation, integrations, and **agent refinement** toward production launch.
- **Align** Product, Engineering, and customer teams on deliverables, timelines, and SLAs.
- **Monitor AI agent performance** in production and drive follow-up improvements.
- Identify and develop **new use cases** with customers to expand adoption and impact.
- **Track progress, manage risk, remove blockers** across the delivery lifecycle.

> This is a **Forward-Deployed Engineer / Strategist** archetype (Palantir-style):
> customer-embedded, technical-but-not-coding, owns outcomes not features.

## Interview process (4–5 rounds)

1. **HR phone call** (15–30 min block) — screening
2. **Case Study Discussion** — deployment scenario
3. **Technical Interview** — leans toward **concepts / frameworks**, not coding
4. **Meeting with the CTO**
5. **Meeting with the General Manager**

## Reading the situation (my analysis)

- **They don't understand the VN market yet** — this is my leverage. I'm not just a
  deployment operator; I'm a VN + SEA enterprise-AI native who can de-risk their
  market entry. Frame myself as *early-team leverage*, not headcount.
- **"Expert with high ownership" + reports to CTO + no GM yet** = the role can be
  shaped. Early ICs in a greenfield market end up defining the playbook. Position
  for scope, not title.
- **Comp is unfixed** — both a risk (no committed band) and an opportunity (I can
  anchor with APAC benchmark + my scarcity as a VN enterprise-AI operator).
- **Startup-mindset signal is explicit** — every round should show *bias to build*,
  comfort with ambiguity, and a "0→1 in a regulated/complex market" track record.
