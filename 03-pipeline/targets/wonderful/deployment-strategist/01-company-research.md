## Product & differentiation

- **Culturally fluent, deeply localized AI agents** — the pitch is that support feels
  native per market, not a translated bot. This is the wedge into **non-English
  markets**, which is Wonderful's stated expansion thesis.
- **One platform, many use cases**, customizable per market (per recruiter: Series A
  built the product; they now have a platform reusable across use cases with
  market-level customization as the unique point).
- Agents handle real transactional work: billing disputes, account updates,
  appointment scheduling — not just deflection/FAQ.
- **Positioning vs. peers** (Decagon, Sierra, Crescendo, Parloa, etc.): the moat they
  claim is **localization + cultural fluency for underserved / non-English markets**,
  not just English-market resolution rate.

## Why this matters for the VN Deployment Strategist role

- **APAC entry is early-2026 and greenfield** — VN is on the "explore" list; per the
  recruiter they *"don't have anything clear about the VN market yet."* Every location
  gets a local team (**GM, CTO, Strategist, FDE, PO, GTM**). The Deployment Strategist
  is a founding-team seat for the market.
- Their **whole thesis is localization** — a candidate who genuinely understands the
  VN enterprise buyer (FMCG, retail, aviation, telecom, tech), the language nuance,
  and the regulatory texture is *directly on-thesis*, not a nice-to-have.
- Hyper-growth + 350→900 hiring = process is being built in real time. **Startup
  mindset** and "ready to build the playbook" is what they're screening for.
